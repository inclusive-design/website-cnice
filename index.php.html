Canadian Network for Inclusive Cultural Exchange (CNICE)

The Canadian Network for Inclusive Cultural Exchange is a group of researchers, developers, producers, disability organizations and artists who have worked together to identify ways that online culture is inaccessible and to provide guidance on how to remove these barriers to access. The results of this collaboration include working draft guidelines documents that are open for discussion in our Guidelines Forum , free tools to make web elements such as videos accessible, and models of practice to demonstrate aspects of accessible culture.



We acknowledge the financial support of the Department of Canadian Heritage through the Canadian Culture Online Program
